INSTRUCTIONS:
1. `observation`: 2-5 short sentences describing what you observe in the
   data. Each sentence cites a specific data point (a return, an RSI
   level, a Form 4 cluster, a headline). Observations are descriptive,
   not interpretive — leave the interpretation for the anchors and
   conviction scores.

2. `bull_anchor`: the SINGLE most load-bearing positive signal you see.
   ≤30 words. NOT a list. If you cannot identify a load-bearing
   positive, write "(none identified)" — that itself is a signal.

3. `bear_anchor`: the SINGLE most load-bearing negative signal you see.
   ≤30 words. NOT a list. Same "(none identified)" escape applies.

4. `what_would_change`: 3-5 SPECIFIC, OBSERVABLE triggers that would
   change your read. Each must be a concrete threshold the operator
   could check. Examples (good):
     - "RSI breaks below 60 on weekly close"
     - "Form 4 cluster buy: ≥2 distinct buyers in next 30d"
     - "Q4 earnings miss revenue by 5%+"
     - "VIX closes above 22 (regime shift confirmed)"
   Examples (bad — too vague):
     - "if fundamentals deteriorate"
     - "more clarity on guidance"
     - "watch for management changes"

5. `conviction`: four independent scores in [0.0, 1.0]:
     - `technical`: trend, momentum, RSI, volume confirmation
     - `fundamental`: earnings trajectory, segment growth, balance sheet,
       insider activity, institutional flow
     - `catalyst`: imminent or recent named catalysts (earnings, FDA,
       legal, M&A, FOMC)
     - `regime`: alignment with current world_state regime + sector
       rotation
   Score each independently. Composite is computed CLIENT-SIDE from
   these four — do NOT pre-aggregate.

6. `decision_tag`: choose ONE of:
     - `CHASE`: all four dimensions ≥0.5 AND a fresh catalyst or
       momentum is emerging now. Operator should act this session.
     - `WATCH`: balanced read (most dimensions 0.4-0.7), no fresh
       trigger yet, no red flags. Operator monitors; no action this
       session.
     - `PROBE`: middle-of-the-road composite BUT one specific open
       question dominates. Operator should `/probe` that question
       before forming a view.
     - `RESEARCH`: conviction dimensions diverge by ≥0.25 (e.g. tech
       0.8, fund 0.3) — worth `/research` to reconcile the gap.
     - `PASS`: weak data across the board, or extension/parabolic red
       flags dominate. Operator skips.
